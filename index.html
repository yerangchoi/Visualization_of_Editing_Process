There are an estimated 1.7 million homeless youth in the United States.[i] 20,000 homeless people 24 years old and younger live in New York City.[ii] A recent survey of national shelters indicates that 40% of homeless youth self-identify as LGBT.[iii]
Children who have been in foster care are at statistically greater risk of becoming homeless at an earlier age, and to remain homeless for a longer period of time than other youth.[iv]
Only 4,000 beds are available across the country for homeless young adults. Of those, 350—mostly in New York City—are LGBT specific.[v]
Youth living on the streets are more likely to engage in “survival sex”–trading sex to gain food, clothing, drugs, money, or just for a safe place to sleep at night.[vi]
One study places the mortality rate of homeless youth under 30 at thirty times higher that of the national average.[vii] Every year, it is estimated that approximately 5,000 homeless young people will die in the United States because of assault, illness, or suicide.[viii]
